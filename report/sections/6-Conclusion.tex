\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

% = PARAGRAPH 1 - Summary

In this paper, we investigated the performance of a language model across different relation types and categories. We found that while category-level differences in performance were relatively small, there was significant variance at the individual relation level. Notably, the `HinderedBy' and `ObjectUse' relations stood out as particularly strong performers, while other relations showed much weaker performance. Our investigation showed that a more granular analysis can provide valuable insights into the strengths and weaknesses of language models in generating common sense knowledge, which may be obscured by aggregate metrics (like a per-category average).

% = PARAGRAPH 2 - Future work

In future work this analysis could be repeated using human evaluation scores instead of BLEU. As mentioned in Section~\ref{sec:discussion}, BLEU may not be the best metric for evaluating the quality of generated text in this context, and human evaluation could provide a more meaningful estimate of per-relation quality. It would also be interesting to investigate how a more balanced test set, with more examples of the weaker-performing relations, would alter the model's performance specifically for those relations.
