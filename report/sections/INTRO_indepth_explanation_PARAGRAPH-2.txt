Each category contains various relation types that represent different kinds of commonsense knowledge. For example, the social interactions category includes relation types such as "xIntent" (the intent behind an action) and "xEffect" (the effect of an action on the subject). The physical interactions category includes relation types such as "AtLocation" (the location of an object) and "HasProperty" (the properties of an object). The event-centered interactions category includes relation types such as "xNeed" (the needs of a subject before an event) and "xWant" (the desires of a subject after an event). \citet{hwang2021cometatomic} demonstrated that COMET-BART outperforms previous models in generating high-quality commonsense knowledge, as measured by aggregate performance metrics across all relation types combined.
